Bengal Tiger
Majestic apex predator of the jungle

üêÖ About Bengal Tigers

The Bengal Tiger is one of the most magnificent predators in the world. These powerful cats are known for their distinctive orange coat with black stripes and incredible hunting prowess.

Habitat: Tropical forests, grasslands, and mangrove swamps across India and Bangladesh.

Diet: Large mammals including deer, wild boar, and buffalo. Can consume up to 25 pounds of meat in a single feeding.

Conservation Status: Endangered - Only about 2,500 individuals remain in the wild.
Asiatic Lion
The king of the jungle

ü¶Å About Asiatic Lions

The Asiatic Lion is a subspecies of lion found only in India's Gir National Park. These majestic cats are slightly smaller than their African cousins but equally impressive.

Habitat: Dry deciduous forests, grasslands, and scrublands in Gujarat, India.

Diet: Hunt in groups targeting deer, antelope, wild boar, and buffalo with cooperative strategies.

Conservation Status: Critically Endangered - Only about 600 individuals remain.
Indian Elephant
Gentle giants with incredible intelligence

üêò About Indian Elephants

Indian Elephants are gentle giants known for their intelligence, emotional depth, and complex social structures. They play a crucial role as keystone species in their ecosystem.

Habitat: Tropical forests, grasslands, and scrublands across India with seasonal migration patterns.

Diet: Consume up to 300 pounds of vegetation daily, spending 16-18 hours feeding on grasses, bark, and fruits.

Conservation Status: Endangered - About 27,000 individuals face habitat fragmentation threats.
Western Ghat Wild Bison
Powerful herbivores of the mountains

üêÉ About Gaur (Indian Bison)

The Gaur is the largest wild bovine species, found in the dense forests of the Western Ghats. These massive herbivores are impressive in both size and strength.

Habitat: Tropical and subtropical forests with dense vegetation and water sources in the Western Ghats.

Diet: Feed on grasses, shoots, fruits, and bark, playing an important role in seed dispersal.

Conservation Status: Vulnerable - Declining populations due to habitat loss and hunting.
Langur Monkey
Playful primates of the forest

üêí About Langur Monkeys

Langurs are intelligent Old World monkeys known for their long tails and complex social behavior. They have sophisticated communication systems and live in organized troops.

Habitat: Various forest types from tropical rainforests to deciduous woodlands, spending most time in trees.

Diet: Primarily herbivorous, feeding on leaves, fruits, flowers, and bark with specialized multi-chambered stomachs.

Conservation Status: Varies by species - Important seed dispersers facing habitat fragmentation.
Indian Leopard
Elusive spotted cats of the night

üêÜ About Indian Leopards

Indian Leopards are solitary, nocturnal cats known for their adaptability and stealth. They are excellent climbers and often store their prey in trees to avoid other predators.

Habitat: Highly adaptable, found in forests, grasslands, mountains, and even urban areas throughout India.

Diet: Opportunistic predators hunting deer, monkeys, birds, and smaller mammals with powerful bite and stealth.

Conservation Status: Near Threatened - Facing habitat loss and human-wildlife conflict.
Indian Peacock
Spectacular birds with vibrant plumage

ü¶ö About Indian Peacocks

The Indian Peacock is the national bird of India, famous for its spectacular tail feathers and vibrant courtship displays. Males display their magnificent plumage to attract females during mating season.

Habitat: Forests, farmlands, and areas near water sources, preferring open woodlands with scattered trees.

Diet: Omnivorous, feeding on insects, small reptiles, seeds, fruits, helping control pests and disperse seeds.

Conservation Status: Stable globally but local pressures from habitat loss exist.
Spotted Deer
Graceful herbivores of the grasslands

ü¶å About Spotted Deer (Chital)

Spotted Deer are beautiful herbivores with distinctive white spots on their reddish-brown coat. They are one of the most common deer species in India and important prey for large predators.

Habitat: Grasslands, open woodlands, and forest edges with good visibility and water access.

Diet: Graze on grasses and browse on leaves, shoots, and fruits, serving as crucial prey for tigers and leopards.

Conservation Status: Generally stable but face habitat loss and livestock competition.
